# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Stranded drivers across the Greater Toronto Area (Toronto/Scarborough, Durham, York, and Peel — home base and fastest response in the East GTA) — most arrive from Google Ads or local search on a phone, at the roadside, often stressed and in a hurry (flat tire, dead battery, lockout). Secondary: researchers comparing roadside options before an emergency happens.

## Product Purpose

Marketing site for iFAST Roadside & Mobile Tires. Its one job is converting visitors into phone calls to +1 437-215-3468. Blog/story pages exist to win local SEO queries and pass trust + internal-link authority to the service ("money") pages. Success = tracked call conversions (Google Ads) and local rankings.

## Brand Personality

Fast, trustworthy, no-nonsense. Big, punchy, conversion-optimized: yellow-on-dark CTAs, oversized type, urgency cues. Warm and human in stories (real technicians, real customers, real reviews) — never corporate or sober.

## Anti-references

- Sober, understated corporate sites (explicitly ruled out in CLAUDE.md — doesn't fit the conversion tone)
- Big-tow-company bureaucracy vibes (call centers, memberships, waiting)
- Generic AI-generated landing-page grammar (endless identical card grids, decorative clutter that buries the phone number)

## Design Principles

- The phone call is the conversion: every screen keeps a call CTA within reach, and nothing may compete with or bury it.
- Readable under stress: a stranded driver on a phone in the sun must be able to skim any page and get the answer + the number fast.
- Real proof over polish: verbatim Google reviews, real names, real locations — authenticity is the differentiator vs. big-brand competitors.
- Match the existing system: brand tokens (brand-dark / brand-yellow), premium-shadow utilities, established section patterns — consistency across pages beats novelty.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG mandate; treat WCAG 2.1 AA as the bar (contrast ≥4.5:1 body text, touch-friendly targets — the audience is overwhelmingly mobile). Respect prefers-reduced-motion for the pulse/shimmer animations.
